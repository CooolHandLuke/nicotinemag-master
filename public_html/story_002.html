ARTIST FEATURE

Littered MVMNTS

Works by

Shoji Yamasaki

Littered MVMNTS is a split-screen video series comprised of found pieces of litter paired with performer, Shoji Yamasaki, mimicking its costume and movements. This project intersects with various themes surrounding value. While beliefs about the value of objects differs between people, cultures, and time, the production of trash unites the human race. We have stepped into an era where we must all learn how to coexist with man-made waste.


Set in the natural soundscape of where the litter was found, the ‘dancing debris’ acts as a form of silent protest to consumer culture and the lack of care given to the environment. By shifting the viewer’s perspective on litter, Yamasaki hopes to contribute to a cultural shift where environmental responsibility is prioritized and becomes an intrinsic value for those living on the sole planet we call home.


Shoji Yamasaki is a first-generation Japanese American transdisciplinary creative, born and raised in the unceded ancestral land of Gabrielino-Tongva, recently known as Los Angeles. He creates site-responsive and immersive works that explore the history, architecture, and community that inhabit a space. His continual desire for expansion as an artist and his experimental ideas have allowed him to take on roles of a choreographer, dancer, performance artist, director, writer, and filmmaker. His on-going project, Littered MVMNTS, has connected with millions of viewers around the world through social media.
