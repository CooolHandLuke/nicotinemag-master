PERSONAL PROJECT

The Dezerter Bazaar,Â Tbilisi

Photography by

Luke Stoychoff

My girlfriend and I left New York City in April during the pandemic to spend three months in The Republic of Georgia. This country, formerly being a part of the Soviet Union, has suffered tremendously over the last thirty years. Though Georgia is suffering in terms of politics it still remains one of the most culturally rich Eastern European countries. I took these three months to document one of its most important landmarks in the city of Tbilisi, the Dezerter Bazaar. This food market has kept families alive for decades and continues to have a massive network of travelers and sellers keeping the market alive and thriving even in the midst of a pandemic.


The market feels energized when you enter, with venders yelling in Russian or Georgian to grab your attention. The interactions I had with the people where so incredible, and being in the market itself was unpredictable and exciting. There was moment where I took a mans photo, then was given a shot of Chacha (Homemade Georgian brandy) and handed a tomato covered in exotic spices to chase the strong drink with. Moments later I walked to the other side of the market, and was bellowed at in Russian by a huge Georgian man as I asked to take his photo, to only then by another vender, be shown a dog he was protecting that had recently given birth with her puppies who were curled up drinking milk from their mother.
